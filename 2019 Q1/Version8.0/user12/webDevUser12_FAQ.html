What is a phobia?
It is an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something.
What is the difference between a phobia and a fear?
When a person has a phobia, there typically appears to be no reason why they have it. It is completely unreasonable and is considered a mental disorder. Phobias can even lead to people not going outside in an attempt to stay away from what causes them extreme panic. A fear, however, is much less intense. People with a fear of spiders will just be jittery or suprised when a spider crawls up their arm, but someone with arachnophobia might have a panic attack.

What are the most common phobias?
Arachnophobia: The fear of spiders Ophidiophobia: The fear of snakes Acrophobia: The fear of heights
How common is it to have a phobia?
Around 11% of Americans have phobias.
