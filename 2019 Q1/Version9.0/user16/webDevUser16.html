How to Play Big Chords

  1. Warm up your hands! Play some scales.
  2. Proceed to dislocate every joint in your fingers.
  3. That's it. That's the only way you can play big chords.
  4. Your hands are going to be jacked for the rest of your life and you just have to accept it.

Actual Tips for Playing Big Chords

  • Flatten your hands! You'll be surprised at how far you can reach when your wrist isn't arching upward.
  • Make sure your hands are loose. A stiff hand is both uncomfortable and unhelpful.
  • Depending on the song, don't try to hold onto the chord. Once you play it, just let the pedal do the rest of the work.
  • Leave out notes if you have to. For octaves, leave off one note. For chords with three or more notes, play the outermost ones and any inner notes you can reach.
  • If reaching any of the notes is impossible, roll all of them.
